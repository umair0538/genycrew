Are you hiring the right candidate?

When you hire the wrong person, you will likely find yourself looking for ways to reassign the employee or trying ways to fit him or her in your organization. This drains a lot of energy and time and costs your business in a number of ways. You are not only paying salary to someone who is not performing to your expectation, but you might even have to pay for additional trainings. In many cases, a bad hire does not get along with other team members which can cause additional problems. Finally, letting go an employee can damage your reputation which in turn impacts your business in a negative way.

You need a very detailed assessment of the candidate in order to avoid the cost of hiring the wrong person. This includes making sure he or she has the required skill set and attitude to get the job done. With so many technological advances, you need someone who will be able to learn and grow with time. Assessing all of this in a job interview can be difficult but you can get a fairly good idea of the candidate by asking the right questions.
